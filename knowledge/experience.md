# Work Experience

## Application Developer at RKL eSolutions (April 2023 – Present)

RKL eSolutions is a technology consulting firm that specializes in Sage ERP implementations and customizations. Jacob joined as an Application Developer in April 2023 and continues in this role.

In this position, Jacob builds custom modifications and integrations for Sage X3 clients across distribution and manufacturing industries. He has delivered more than 100 custom Sage X3 modifications — from minor configuration changes to entirely new ERP modules — and more than 10 production integrations. Some of these customizations process hundreds of client invoices daily. His day-to-day work involves scoping and delivering development projects, working directly with clients throughout: requirements gathering, design reviews, debugging in live environments, and ongoing support for a client base that ranges from highly technical engineers to non-technical business owners.

Notable integration work includes third-party connectors with platforms such as ShipStation (shipping automation), Centage (budgeting and planning), Optimal Answers (optimization tools), and iDoc (document management). These integrations use a variety of technologies depending on what the platform requires: Adonix 4GL consuming REST APIs for ERP-side operations, .NET for robust service-layer work, PowerShell for automation scripts, and SFTP for file-based data exchange.

The ShipStation integration is a particular standout. Jacob designed and built it end-to-end: it syncs Sage X3 deliveries to ShipStation orders and fetches tracking numbers back into the ERP, handling high-volume production use (hundreds of shipments daily). He then wrote the technical blog post that drives customer discovery, and RKL now sells the integration as a product with a fixed fee and recurring maintenance revenue — about five clients use it as of mid-2026.

Jacob also built a custom browser extension that extracts data directly from Sage X3 — a system that previously had no effective export path — enabling downstream integrations that weren't previously possible.

In 2026, Jacob began building an internal project management and collaboration platform for RKL. The stack is React and Vite on the frontend, with a REST API and integrations to ITGlue and Salesforce, using GitHub for identity. The defining feature is an MCP (Model Context Protocol) server that allows users to query their data and the application itself through Claude Desktop or the company chatbot — making the software self-documenting and self-explaining without requiring formal onboarding. Development is Claude Code-assisted.

Jacob also supports the broader consulting and technical teams on advanced Sage X3 issues, and places a high value on thorough documentation — both for client-facing deliverables and internal knowledge transfer.

RKL eSolutions was also where Jacob did his internships (see below), so his relationship with the company goes back to 2018.

## SQL Report Writer and Data Analyst at Gaming Laboratories International (November 2022 – April 2023)

Gaming Laboratories International (GLI) is one of the world's leading gaming testing and certification labs. Jacob worked there in a data-focused role, building and maintaining reporting infrastructure for the organization.

During his time at GLI, Jacob built and maintained a portfolio of more than 20 SSRS (SQL Server Reporting Services) reports, SSIS (SQL Server Integration Services) packages, and Power BI dashboards. He wrote highly optimized T-SQL queries and complex stored procedures to power reporting across the organization, and built data pipelines to support operational reporting.

A significant part of this role involved designing executive dashboards — visualizations that gave leadership a clear, real-time view of business performance. Jacob worked closely with stakeholders to understand what metrics mattered and translate those into clear, actionable dashboards.

This role gave Jacob deep experience in the Microsoft BI stack (SSMS, SSRS, SSIS, Power BI, PowerQuery) and solidified his skills in SQL query optimization and data pipeline design.

## Test Engineer II at Gaming Laboratories International (July 2021 – November 2022)

Jacob's first full-time professional role was as a Test Engineer II at GLI. In this role, he wrote test plans and performed hands-on testing of slot machines and electronic table games for regulatory compliance. He evaluated gaming devices against jurisdictional standards across a range of hardware configurations.

This role required meticulous attention to detail, the ability to work across complex technical documentation, and the discipline to follow rigorous testing protocols. It gave Jacob a strong foundation in QA methodology and an understanding of regulated software environments. He also built AutoHotKey scripts and custom tooling to automate repetitive test workflows, materially improving team throughput — an early example of his instinct to build tooling around processes that don't yet have it.

A significant part of the role involved high-stakes compliance meetings attended by both clients and regulators simultaneously. Jacob represented both the technical test results and their regulatory implications in real time, to multiple audiences with different stakes — an experience that sharpened his ability to make technically dense information credible and actionable to non-technical decision-makers under pressure.
