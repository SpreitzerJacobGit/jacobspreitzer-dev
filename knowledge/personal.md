# Personal Interests and Hobbies

## Endurance Cycling

Jacob is a serious recreational cyclist. Most weekends he's out for 50 to 80 miles on whatever mix of road and gravel looks good on the map. He doesn't race and doesn't track Strava segments — the appeal is the miles themselves, the physical effort, and the occasional gas station snack stop in the middle of nowhere.

He rides both road and gravel, and his route selection tends to favor routes that are visually interesting over routes that are fast or flat. The endurance aspect is central — longer rides that require pacing and nutrition management, not short hard efforts.

Cycling is Jacob's main physical outlet and a significant part of his weekly routine.

## Table Tennis

Jacob has been playing table tennis seriously for a couple of years and is fully invested in the equipment side of the sport. His current setup is a Stiga Legacy Carbon blade paired with Skyline 3 rubbers — a combination that reflects a preference for controlled aggression rather than pure speed.

He actively seeks out competitive games. This is a polite way of saying he will challenge basically anyone he sees near a table. He's interested in improving and plays against anyone willing, regardless of level — though he genuinely enjoys finding players who push him.

Jacob is familiar with the table tennis equipment ecosystem and can talk at length about blade construction, rubber characteristics, and the tradeoffs involved in equipment selection.

## Chinese Tea

Jacob has a full gongfu tea setup and a strong preference for pu'erh, particularly ripe (shou) pu'erh as his daily drinker. He has proper teaware and approaches brewing with care — precise steeping times, water temperature attention, multiple short steeps from the same leaves.

The gongfu tea practice is deliberately slow. Twenty minutes of doing nothing except paying attention to tea and watching the color of the liquor in a small cup is, in Jacob's view, genuinely good for you. It's a form of focused attention that doesn't involve screens or productivity.

He also drinks other Chinese teas — sheng (raw) pu'erh, oolongs, and others — but ripe pu'erh is the consistent go-to.

## Personal Productivity and AI

Jacob builds complex spreadsheets for basically everything in his personal life: personal finances, fitness tracking, cycling stats, gear inventories, and whatever else needs a proper tracking system. He enjoys the design work involved in building a good spreadsheet — clear structure, the right formulas, useful views.

He's also a heavy Claude user and is genuinely enthusiastic about finding new ways to get value out of AI in daily life — both professionally and personally. This extends beyond the obvious use cases; he's interested in the craft of prompting well, building AI-augmented workflows, and understanding where AI actually helps versus where it's noise.

His interest in personal productivity is practical rather than aspirational — he actually uses the systems he builds.
